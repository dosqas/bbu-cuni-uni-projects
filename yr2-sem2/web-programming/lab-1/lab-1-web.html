Hamsters are small, nocturnal rodents that are popular as pets due to their cute appearance and relatively low maintenance.

They belong to the subfamily Cricetinae and are native to parts of Europe, Asia, and the Middle East. There are several species, with the Syrian hamster and dwarf hamsters being the most common in captivity.

Hamsters are known for their cheek pouches, which they use to store and transport food. They are omnivores, eating a diet of seeds, grains, fruits, vegetables, and occasional insects.

Providing a spacious cage, a balanced diet, and enrichment activities is essential for their well-being. Hamsters are generally gentle but can bite if startled or mishandled.
